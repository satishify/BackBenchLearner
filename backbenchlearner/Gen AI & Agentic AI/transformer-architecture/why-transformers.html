Why Transformers Replaced Earlier Seq2Seq Models

Before Transformers, sequence tasks used RNN/LSTM encoder-decoder models. They worked, but had major scaling and context limitations.

Main limitations of pre-attention and RNN seq2seq

  • Fixed context bottleneck: whole input compressed into one vector.
  • Weak long-range memory: distant tokens lose influence.
  • Sequential processing: poor parallelism, slower training.
  • Vanishing/exploding gradients: optimization instability.
  • Uniform treatment: no dynamic focus on most relevant words.
flowchart LR A[RNN Encoder] --> B[Single context vector] B --> C[RNN Decoder] C --> D[Output sequence] B -. bottleneck .-> E[Information loss on long input]

Attention solved the bottleneck first

Attention allowed each decoder step to look at all encoder hidden states with dynamic weights, instead of relying only on one fixed vector.

Why Transformer was the next leap

  • Removes recurrence and enables full parallel token processing.
  • Self-attention gives direct token-to-token interaction.
  • Dependency path between far tokens becomes short (direct).
  • Multi-head attention captures different relation types simultaneously.
