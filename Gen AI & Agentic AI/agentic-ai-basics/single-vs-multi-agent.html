Single-Agent vs Multi-Agent Systems

Single-agent

  • One agent handles all tasks end to end.
  • Simpler architecture and easier debugging.
  • Good for moderate complexity workflows.

Multi-agent

  • Multiple specialized agents collaborate.
  • Useful for complex pipelines: planner, researcher, reviewer.
  • Needs orchestration and clear handoff contracts.

Decision guide

Start with single-agent. Move to multi-agent when tasks have clear specialization and scaling needs.
