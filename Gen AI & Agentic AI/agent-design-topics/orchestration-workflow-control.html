Orchestration and Workflow Control

Orchestration controls how agent steps are executed: order, retries, fallback, timeouts, and approvals.

Core controls

  • Step sequencing and dependencies.
  • Retry policy and exponential backoff.
  • Timeout limits and circuit breaking.
  • Fallback model/tool selection.
  • Human approval checkpoints.

Example

If primary search API fails twice, fallback to backup index and continue workflow.

Why it matters in production

  • Makes systems predictable under failures.
  • Prevents infinite loops and uncontrolled spending.
  • Improves observability and governance.
